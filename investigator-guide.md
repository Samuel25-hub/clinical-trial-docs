# Investigator Guide

## Study Overview

This fictional Phase II clinical trial evaluates the safety and effectiveness of HX-214, an investigational therapy for patients with moderate inflammatory disease symptoms.

The study is designed as a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial conducted across multiple clinical research sites.

---

## Study Objectives

### Primary Objective

Evaluate the effectiveness of HX-214 in reducing symptom severity over a 12-week treatment period.

### Secondary Objectives

- Assess treatment safety and tolerability
- Monitor adverse event frequency
- Evaluate participant quality-of-life outcomes
- Measure treatment adherence rates

---

## Study Design

| Study Element | Description |
|---|---|
| Phase | Phase II |
| Design | Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled |
| Duration | 12 Weeks |
| Participants | 120 |
| Study Sites | Multi-site |
| Treatment Groups | HX-214 and Placebo |

---

## Eligibility Criteria

### Inclusion Criteria

Participants must:

- Be between 18 and 65 years old
- Provide informed consent
- Meet diagnostic criteria for moderate inflammatory disease
- Agree to study visit schedules

### Exclusion Criteria

Participants may not:

- Be enrolled in another investigational study
- Have uncontrolled chronic illness
- Be pregnant or breastfeeding
- Have known allergy to investigational therapy components

---

## Study Workflow

### Screening Visit

- Verify eligibility
- Review medical history
- Obtain informed consent
- Perform baseline assessments

### Treatment Phase

Participants receive investigational treatment and attend scheduled follow-up visits every two weeks.

### Final Evaluation

- Complete final safety assessment
- Review adverse events
- Perform outcome measurements

---

## Safety Monitoring

Investigators must monitor participants for:

- adverse events
- serious adverse events
- treatment reactions
- protocol deviations

All safety events should be documented according to study reporting procedures.

---

## Investigator Responsibilities

Investigators are responsible for:

- maintaining protocol compliance
- ensuring participant safety
- documenting study activities accurately
- reporting adverse events promptly
- protecting participant confidentiality

---

## Documentation Requirements

The following records must be maintained:

- informed consent forms
- case report forms
- adverse event documentation
- participant visit records
- protocol deviation records
