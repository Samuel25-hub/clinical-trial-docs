# Data Collection Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

## Purpose

This SOP describes the procedures for collecting, reviewing, and documenting clinical trial data during the HX-214 Phase II study.

The purpose of this procedure is to support accurate, complete, and consistent study data collection.

---

## Scope

This procedure applies to:
- investigators
- research coordinators
- study personnel responsible for data collection and entry

---

## Responsibilities

### Investigators

Investigators are responsible for:
- ensuring data accuracy
- reviewing completed records
- addressing protocol deviations
- maintaining participant confidentiality

### Research Coordinators

Research coordinators are responsible for:
- collecting participant data
- completing case report forms (CRFs)
- entering study information into approved systems
- reporting missing or inconsistent data

---

## Data Collection Workflow

### Step 1: Verify Participant Identity

Before collecting study data:
- confirm participant identification
- verify study enrollment status
- confirm visit schedule

---

### Step 2: Complete Source Documentation

Record:
- participant visit details
- vital signs
- laboratory results
- medication information
- reported symptoms
- adverse events

All entries must be accurate and completed promptly.

---

### Step 3: Complete Case Report Forms (CRFs)

Study personnel must:
- complete all required CRF sections
- use approved terminology
- avoid blank required fields
- correct errors according to study procedures

---

### Step 4: Review Data for Accuracy

Review collected data for:
- missing information
- incorrect dates
- inconsistent measurements
- incomplete documentation

Issues should be corrected before final submission.

---

### Step 5: Submit Data

Submit completed documentation according to study timelines and sponsor requirements.

---

## Data Correction Procedures

If corrections are needed:
- maintain original entries when possible
- document reason for correction
- include initials and correction date
- avoid unauthorized changes

---

## Confidentiality Requirements

Study personnel must:
- protect participant confidentiality
- avoid unauthorized disclosure of participant information
- follow organizational privacy and security procedures

---

## Documentation Retention

Study records should be retained according to:
- sponsor requirements
- institutional policies
- regulatory retention guidelines
